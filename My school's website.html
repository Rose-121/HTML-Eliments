Faridpur Gov't Girls' High School

Faridpur Government Girls' High School is an ancient and traditional educational institution in Bangladesh. Faridpur Government Girls' High School was established in 1910 [1] and has been playing a unique role in the promotion of girls' education in Faridpur district. It is another best institution for women's education. As the oldest school of the century, this educational institution is a unique beacon of light, a pioneering educational institution with its own characteristics. Every year, the students of this school pass the Primary Final Examination, J.S.C. Examination and S.S.C. Examination with distinction.And has a 100% pass rate.
The students here excel beyond measure, their accomplishments forming a legacy that knows no bounds. The school's collection of awards is so vast, it defies enumeration—a true testament to its culture of brilliance.

History

  • Establishment The school, established in 1910, was named Government Middle Vernacular Girls’ School, Faridpur. It was then a primary school. In 1917, the word Vernacular was dropped from the school’s name and it was changed to Government Middle Girls’ School. Over time, in 1950, it was upgraded to a secondary school and became known as Faridpur Govt. Girls' High School. Later, admission of students in 1st, 2nd, and 3rd classes was stopped. Since 1991, two shifts have been operating in this school. Currently, the number of students in this school is about 3000. There are 59 teachers. And 12 employees. The school is divided into two shifts. A total of 3000 students are studying. The centenary of this school was celebrated in 2010.

Building
